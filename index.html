Our Story

Built by graduates, for the town that raised them.

In 1990, a group of local graduates founded the Muslim Graduates Educational Society with a direct aim: to make good schooling reachable for every child in Lalpet, regardless of what their family could pay. Imam Gazzali School began as a modest institution and grew, year by year, into a full Matriculation Higher Secondary school — today one of the largest in Lalpet and Cuddalore district, with three wings serving boys, girls, and young children from Nursery age.

The school takes its name from Imam al-Ghazali, the 11th-century scholar whose best-known work is titled, in translation, The Niche of Lights — a meditation on how knowledge illuminates the way forward. It's a fitting name for a school in Lalpet, a town with its own long relationship with learning.

Lalpet was established in 1775 by Lal Khan, an administrator under the Nawab of Arcot, as a settlement where Arab traders and people of different faiths could live and do business together. Less than a century later, in 1862, the town became home to Jamia Manba'ul Anwar — literally, the "Source of Lights" — among the oldest Arabic colleges in Tamil Nadu. Imam Gazzali School carries that same thread forward for a new generation, in a town whose literacy rate today runs well above the national average.

Mission & Values

What our motto means for every classroom.

رَبِّ زِدْنِي عِلْمًا

"Oh God, increase me in knowledge."

Qur'an 20:114 — Surah Ta-Ha

This prayer, asking God for ever-increasing knowledge, is the line inscribed on our school seal — in Arabic, English, and Tamil. It's more than an emblem. It's the standard the Muslim Graduates Educational Society set when it founded this school in 1990, and the standard every wing is still measured against.

In practice, that means a classroom where knowledge is treated as something to keep asking for — through exam results, yes, but also through character, discipline, and a sense of duty to the community a student comes from.
